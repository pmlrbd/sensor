Physical Sensor Authority

The Hallucination
Firewall for
Multimodal LLMs

SensorBabel is a lightweight inference-time module that routes MLLM claims to their physically authoritative sensors—LiDAR, Radar, Thermal, Depth—and suppresses hallucinations through sensor-independent verification. No model weights touched.

Architecture

Four-Stage Inference
Pipeline

SensorBabel wraps any frozen MLLM at inference time. It extracts verifiable claims, routes each to its physically authoritative sensor, collects a verdict, and re-weights the decoding distribution to suppress inconsistent claims.

STAGE 01
📝
Claim Extractor
Parses MLLM output into atomic subject–predicate–object triples, assigns semantic type, confidence κⱼ, and claim embedding.
Theorem 3.3
STAGE 02
🔀
Sensor Routing Policy
A 3-layer MLP maps claim embeddings to probability distributions over sensors using physics-based authority scores.
Theorem 3.4
STAGE 03a
⚖️
Verdict Networks
Five sensor-specific 2-layer MLPs score claim–sensor consistency. Each trains only on its authoritative claim type.
STAGE 03b
🔧
Contrastive Re-weighting
Verdict scores re-weight the output distribution. Sensor-inconsistent claims are suppressed with probability σ(λ(0.5−vⱼ)).
Theorem 3.5
OUTPUT
✅
Hallucination-Free Output
Suppressed claims removed from output. Underlying MLLM weights remain untouched throughout.

Sensor Authority

Physically Orthogonal
Verification Channels

Each sensor operates on independent physical principles, making them immune to the statistical biases that drive language-model hallucination. Theorem 3.3 formalizes this as the Sensor Independence Firewall.

🔵
LiDAR
EXISTENCE CLAIMS
Photon time-of-flight ranging provides direct evidence of object presence within a scene. Binary presence/absence in a 3×3m bounding box gives the cleanest verification signal of all sensor types.

Radar
MOTION CLAIMS
Doppler frequency shift provides radial velocity measurements that directly adjudicate claims about object speed and direction. Even slow-moving objects near minimum detectable velocity retain useful signal.

Stereo Depth
SPATIAL CLAIMS
Stereo disparity maps provide metric distance estimates to spatial anchors in the scene. Calibrated depth maps resolve "5 meters ahead" or "immediately adjacent" claims that language models frequently confabulate.

MATERIAL CLAIMS
Blackbody emissivity profiles uniquely identify material categories through surface temperature signatures. Metal, glass, vegetation, and rubber each have distinct thermal fingerprints invisible to RGB cameras.
